# Overview: What is SFI?

## Definition

**Swedish for Immigrants (SFI)** is an education within municipal adult education (komvux) that provides adult immigrants with basic knowledge of the Swedish language and Swedish society.

## Purpose and Goals

SFI aims to:

- Provide basic knowledge of the Swedish language
- Convey knowledge about Swedish society
- Facilitate establishment in the labor market
- Enable continued studies

## Who Has the Right to SFI?

A person has the right to SFI if they:

1. **Have a residence permit** in Sweden
2. **Are registered** in a Swedish municipality
3. **Do not speak Swedish**
4. **Are 16 years or older** (second half of the year you turn 16)

### Exceptions

The following persons **do not** have the right to SFI:

- Persons who speak Danish or Norwegian
- Asylum seekers and undocumented persons (until residence permit is granted)

## Municipality's Responsibility

The municipality is obliged to:

- Offer SFI to eligible persons
- Start the education within **three months** of registration
- Arrange the education free of charge

## Education Obligation for Newly Arrived

Since **January 1, 2018**, there is an education obligation for newly arrived persons. This means:

- The Public Employment Service (Arbetsförmedlingen) can refer newly arrived persons to SFI
- Participation is required to receive establishment allowance
- The state finances the education for refugees discharged from the Swedish Migration Agency's reception system

## Structure of SFI

The education consists of:

- **Four courses**: A, B, C, and D
- **Three study paths**: Adapted to different prerequisites
- **Civic orientation**: Included in the education

## Statistics

- **2013**: 113,600 persons studied SFI
- **Percentage of beginners**: Four out of ten (2013)
- **Pass rate**: One third pass at the lower course levels
