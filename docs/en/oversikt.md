# Overview: What is SFI?

## Definition

**Swedish for Immigrants (SFI)** is an education program in Swedish language for adult immigrants who lack basic Swedish skills. The program is regulated by the School Act (Skollagen) and is free of charge for participants.

## Who Has the Right to SFI?

You have the right to SFI if you:

- **Are 16 years or older** (adult education)
- **Lack basic Swedish language skills** (defined as lacking knowledge equivalent to grade 6 level)
- **Are registered in a Swedish municipality** (folkbokförd)

### Priority Groups

Certain groups have priority for SFI placement:

1. **Newly arrived immigrants** — should be offered SFI within 3 months
2. **Refugees and persons in need of protection**
3. **Persons with limited schooling** — may need study path 1

## Municipal Responsibility

Each municipality is obligated to:

- **Provide SFI education** to all eligible residents
- **Assess language skills** through placement testing
- **Offer suitable study paths** based on educational background
- **Provide quality education** following national curricula

### How to Register

Contact your **local municipality's adult education center** (komvux):

1. Visit the municipality website and search for "SFI"
2. Submit an application online or in person
3. Complete a placement test (särvux bedömning)
4. Receive placement in appropriate course and study path

## SFI as a Gateway

SFI serves as a stepping stone to:

| Next Step | Description |
|-----------|-------------|
| **Upper secondary school** | Komvux programs for adults |
| **Higher education** | University/college with Swedish proficiency |
| **Vocational training** | Yrkesvux and labor market programs |
| **Employment** | Swedish language skills are often required |

## Legal Framework

SFI is governed by:

- **School Act (Skollagen)** — chapter 22, sections 1-3
- **SFI Ordinance (SFI-förordningen)** — detailed regulations
- **National Curriculum** — content and learning outcomes
