# Course D within SFI

Course D is the final course within **Study Path 3** and corresponds to the highest level within SFI. After this course, participants have knowledge equivalent to Swedish at upper secondary level.

## Target Group

Course D is for you who:
- Have completed Course C (Study Path 3)
- Have good study capacity
- Are highly educated or have longer schooling
- Want to reach an advanced level in Swedish

## Course Goals

After completing the course, you should be able to:

### Reading
- Read and analyze complex texts
- Understand different text types and genres
- Critically examine sources
- Draw conclusions and interpret underlying messages

### Writing
- Write well-developed texts with clear structure
- Use different text types professionally
- Use advanced grammar and varied vocabulary
- Adapt style and tone to recipient

### Speaking
- Give well-structured oral presentations
- Actively participate in advanced discussions
- Argue convincingly
- Use varied and nuanced language

### Listening
- Understand complex information in speech and media
- Follow advanced discussions and lectures
- Interpret underlying messages and attitudes

## Teaching Format

Course D emphasizes:
- Independent and responsible learning
- Academic writing
- Advanced text analysis
- Oral presentations and rhetoric
- Source criticism

## Content

### Themes
1. **Society and Politics** — Democracy, equality, human rights
2. **Culture and Literature** — Swedish and international culture
3. **Science and Research** — Understanding scientific texts
4. **Professional Life** — Professional communication
5. **Future Plans** — Studies, career, community engagement

### Language Areas
- **Vocabulary**: Advanced and nuanced vocabulary
- **Grammar**: Complex sentences, indirect speech, subjunctive
- **Style**: Academic, formal, and professional language
- **Rhetoric**: Convincing presentation, argumentation techniques

## National Exams

!!! info "National exam exists"
    For Course D, there are national exams issued by the Swedish National Agency for Education.

### Parts of the Exam
- Reading comprehension of complex texts
- Writing task with advanced requirements
- Listening comprehension
- Oral interaction and presentation

## Assessment

### Grades
- **G** — Pass
- **IG** — Fail

### Assessment Criteria
- Ability to communicate in advanced situations
- Linguistic correctness and precision
- Nuanced and varied language use
- Critical thinking and source criticism

## Course Length

Typical study time: **200–400 hours**

Study Path 3 participants can often complete the course faster.

## After Course D

After passing Course D, you can:
- Continue with Swedish as a Second Language (SAS) at upper secondary level
- Start vocational higher education programs
- Study at university (with complementary qualifications)
- Seek work requiring advanced Swedish skills
