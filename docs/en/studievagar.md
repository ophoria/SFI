# Study Paths within SFI

SFI education is offered within **three different study paths**, adapted to participants' different prerequisites and educational backgrounds.

## Overview of Study Paths

| Study Path | Target Group | Courses |
|-----------|--------------|---------|
| **Study Path 1** | Illiterates and persons with very short schooling | A and B |
| **Study Path 2** | Persons with shorter schooling | B and C |
| **Study Path 3** | Persons with longer schooling and study experience | C and D |

## Study Path 1

### Target Group
- Persons who lack reading and writing skills in their mother tongue
- Persons with very short or no schooling (up to approximately 4-6 years)

### Courses
- **Course A**: Basic reading and writing training, oral Swedish
- **Course B**: Continued basic education

### Goals
After completing the study path, participants should be able to:
- Read and write at a basic level
- Handle everyday situations in Swedish
- Understand simple texts and instructions

### Teaching
For illiterates, the teaching includes:
- Basic reading and writing training
- Basic numeracy
- Oral Swedish

## Study Path 2

### Target Group
- Persons with shorter schooling
- Persons with basic reading and writing skills

### Courses
- **Course B**: Continuation from basic level
- **Course C**: Intermediate level

### Goals
After completing the study path, participants should be able to:
- Communicate in everyday situations
- Read and understand simpler texts
- Write simple messages and texts
- Participate in society at a basic level

## Study Path 3

### Target Group
- Persons with longer schooling
- Persons with good study capacity
- Highly educated persons

### Courses
- **Course C**: Intermediate level for study-experienced
- **Course D**: Advanced level

### Goals
After completing the study path, participants should be able to:
- Read and understand complex texts
- Write well-developed texts
- Participate in studies and work in Swedish
- Continue with other adult education or enter the labor market

### Continuation Opportunities
After Course D, participants can:
- Apply for upper secondary completion
- Start vocational higher education
- Seek work requiring good Swedish skills

## Choice of Study Path

### Assessment
At registration, an assessment is made to evaluate:
- Previous schooling
- Reading and writing skills
- Study experience
- Goals for the education

### Reassignment
If a participant proves to be incorrectly placed, reassignment to another study path can be made.

## Civic Orientation

Civic orientation is included in all study paths and covers:
- Structure and function of Swedish society
- Labor market and working life
- Everyday life in Sweden
- Rights and obligations
- Health and welfare
